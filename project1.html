Information Design

- Design offline approach to help residents get notice of extreme heat.

- Inform and direct residents go to the website to learn more when they view the offline approach.

- Design a website to convey the information directly.

The goal of the project is to inform and direct the individuals who view the offline materials to move to the online materials. It’s a project for group of 2. All of the design and prototype are made by ourselves. My roles is web design and graphic design.

Offline Approach

We design a double side fan with our slogan and our website address on it. We design a fan because people need fan when they feel hot. We can allocate them on street to people and most people are willing to accept it. So that they have chance to get access to our website.

We use orange and blue as main color. Orange represent hot and blue represent cold. Our website address is on blue side. “Please click our website to learn more about how to get colder when you face extreme heat.”

Wireframe

- Made the website responsive with an adaptive grid structure

- Relevant images and videos included

- Implemented click to reveal text (show more/less, Q&A display)

- Visual hierarchy present through headings h1, h2, h3, h4, etc.

- Navigable elements clearly seen by changing colour on hover and having different coloured text

- Can be displayed on any device

- Clear illustration.

Challenge

The biggest challenge of this project is our weak coding skill. Some design aesthetic in our mind that we are unable using coding to display it on our interface. For example we want to make a hamburger menu button when responsive to mobile size but we cannot do that at that time. And we didn't learn javascript at that time. We cannot realize some interaction with user in the page. Flexbox and grid is also a challenge for us, we only make some easy layout to display the data we collect.
